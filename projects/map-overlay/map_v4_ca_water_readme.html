CA water runoff `map_v4_ca_water.html`

A simple, stable “height + water” cellular automaton over a MapLibre map, driven by Terrarium DEM tiles.

What you’re looking at

  • Terrain: a Terrarium DEM mosaic (currently 5×5 tiles) is loaded around the map center at a fixed zoom level.
  • Grid: the simulation runs on an N×N grid (the Grid slider). Each cell stores a water amount W.
  • Mapping: each CA cell corresponds to a screen position; that screen position is unprojected to lon/lat and then converted into DEM pixel space.
  • Valid mask: cells outside the DEM mosaic are marked invalid and don’t simulate (render transparent). This avoids edge “clamp” artifacts.

State

  • H: terrain height (meters), sampled from the DEM for each cell.
  • W: water amount (unitless mass), per cell.

Rendering

  • Cells are colored by a heatmap ramp based on W (with a soft-saturating scale).
  • When “Debug cells” is enabled, it draws per-cell values of W (slow on large grids).
  • When zoomed in, it switches to nearest-neighbor sampling to reduce moiré/scanline artifacts.

Dynamics (the CA rule)

Each step, every valid cell gets rainfall and then redistributes some of its water to lower neighbors.

  • Rain: every step adds baseline rain to each cell. With oscillation enabled, the rain level is max(0, Rain + RainAmp·sin(2π·t/RainPeriod)). The bottom-right bar shows “Rain now”.
  • Flow routing: water is routed downhill based on H (terrain height), not H+W.
  • Neighborhood: a radius R neighborhood (3×3 up to 9×9) is used. Lower cells within radius receive water proportional to (drop / distance).
  • Pooling bias: outflow is reduced in flatter areas using a “steepness” factor so low valleys can retain more water.
  • Evap: after redistribution, water is multiplied by (1 - Evap).

Brush rain

  • Click Brush near the rain meter to open the brush popout.
  • Enabling the brush disables map interactions so click-drag paints water cleanly.
  • Brush size: radius in cells.
  • Amount: how strongly water is added under the brush (with radial falloff).

Controls & tips

  • Reset water: equalizes W across all valid cells (uniform starting state).
  • Reload DEM: reloads the DEM mosaic around the current map center (use after big pans).
  • d: toggles slope debug visualization.
  • If you see artifacts at the edges, it usually means you’ve panned outside the currently loaded DEM mosaic—hit Reload DEM.

Advect (D8) — v6 only

In map_v6_ca_water_gpu.html, each CA step is a two-pass update on the water texture. First, a mild surface-leveling term spreads water to neighbors using height differences (similar in spirit to the CPU model’s lateral routing). Second, D8 advection moves water along the drainage network derived from the sampled terrain.

  • Flow directions: from the same H grid used for the sim, the CPU builds a D8 flow direction per cell (steepest descent) and a flow accumulation field used for the optional FA overlay. The CA shader reads the direction texture to know “downhill” for each cell.
  • Slider “Advect (D8)” (internal name faBoost) is converted for the fragment shader as u_advect = faBoost / 20. The on-screen number is therefore 20× the normalized advect strength, which is clamped in the shader to 0…2.
  • Default faBoost = 30 ⇒ u_advect = 1.5. That sets how aggressively water follows the D8 paths each step: push a fraction advect × Worig out along your downslope direction (using pre-rain W), and pull the same fraction of neighbors’ pre-step water when their D8 arrow points into your cell.
  • 0 turns off D8 transfer (only the leveling pass remains). Higher values tighten runoff into channels; very high values can move large fractions of W per step, so balance with Flow, Rain, and Steps/frame.
